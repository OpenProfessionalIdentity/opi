# Open Professional Identity (OPI) Manifesto

## Publish once. Be understood everywhere.

### Why OPI exists

Professional identity is fragmented.

Today, a person's experience is spread across LinkedIn, GitHub, personal websites, portfolios, resumes, freelance platforms, certifications, blogs, publications, and many other sources.

Keeping these profiles synchronized is difficult for professionals.

Understanding them is even harder for recruiters, clients, companies, and AI systems.

As artificial intelligence becomes part of recruitment, consulting, business development, and professional networking, the problem becomes even bigger. AI systems are forced to reconstruct a person's experience from disconnected, inconsistent, and incomplete information.

There should be a better way.

---

## Our vision

Open Professional Identity (OPI) is an open standard for publishing professional knowledge.

It enables professionals to aggregate their public professional information into a single structured identity that can be understood by both humans and AI systems.

OPI does not replace LinkedIn, GitHub, personal websites, resumes, or portfolios.

It connects them.

The professional remains the owner of their identity.

---

## Two types of users

### Publishers

Professionals who connect their existing profiles, curate their knowledge, and publish a trusted professional identity.

Publishers maintain one source of truth instead of manually updating multiple disconnected profiles.

### Consumers

Humans and software systems that want to understand a professional.

Consumers may include recruiters, headhunters, employers, clients, investors, conference organizers, marketplaces, ATS systems, and AI agents.

Instead of searching through multiple websites and documents, they consume a single professional identity.

---

## Our principles

* Professionals own their data.
* Existing platforms remain valuable.
* OPI connects professional knowledge instead of replacing it.
* Human-readable and AI-readable representations should always coexist.
* The standard should be open, portable, transparent, and extensible.
* AI should help understand professional identity, not define it.

---

## Our mission

To create an open ecosystem where every professional can publish a portable, trustworthy, AI-readable identity that can be consumed by humans, applications, and intelligent agents.

---

## Our promise

Publish once.

Be understood everywhere.
