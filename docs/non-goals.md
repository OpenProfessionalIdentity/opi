# What OPI is NOT

One of the primary goals of Open Professional Identity is to remain focused.

OPI is designed as an open standard and a reference implementation for publishing AI-readable professional identities.

Anything outside that scope should be considered carefully before becoming part of the project.

## OPI is NOT a social network

OPI does not replace LinkedIn or other professional networking platforms.

## OPI is NOT a recruiting platform

OPI helps professionals publish their identities and enables consumers to understand them.

Recruitment workflows belong to recruiting platforms.

## OPI is NOT an Applicant Tracking System (ATS)

ATS vendors may consume OPI, but OPI is not an ATS.

## OPI is NOT a portfolio builder

Portfolio websites may use OPI as a source of truth, but OPI is not a website builder.

## OPI is NOT an AI provider

OPI does not depend on OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, or any specific AI model.

Any AI system should be able to consume OPI.

## OPI is NOT a proprietary cloud service

Professionals should be free to self-host, use cloud providers, or export their identity.

## OPI is NOT another copy of your LinkedIn profile

OPI aggregates professional knowledge from multiple trusted sources and presents it as one portable professional identity.

## OPI is NOT owned by a central authority

Professional identity belongs to the professional.

The OPI specification is open.

Anyone can implement it.
